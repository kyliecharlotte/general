Projects:

This list contains links to projects that I have developed both alone and with a larger group.
  1. easyA - Group project for CS 422 (Software Methodologies) at the University of Oregon.

    easyA was designed to let students visualize the grade distribution for different classes across the UO. I built the web scraper and administrative tools responsible for updating the data used.

    Link: easyA

  2. Dollarless Dining - Group project for CS 422 (Software Methodologies) at the University of Oregon.

    Dollarless Dining is a visualization and information tool for students at the UO to view upcoming free food resources and where they are found. I came up with the concept, built the web scraper, and developed admin tools for the updating of the food information.

    Link: Dollarless Dining

  3. Concert Scan - Solo Project for CS 407 (Career Internships Seminar) at the University of Oregon.

    Concert Scan allows users to either enter an artist's name, select a venue, or select a date range, to view upcoming events in Eugene relating to their choice. This was a personal project I created to work with GUIs and web scraping.

    Link: Concert Scan
Research:

In June of 2024, I will be attending an 8-week summer research program (REU) at Georgetown University in Washington, DC.

This REU will utilize incoming misinformation detection techniques to analyze multiple information hubs (surveys, social media, newspaper articles) leading up to the 2024 election. I will be working under Dr. Lisa Singh, who is a Professor of Computer Science at Georgetown who specializes in data-centric computing. Additionally, the program features more Georgetown CS faculty members as mentors, including Dr. Sarah Bargal and Dr. Micah Sherr.

I am excited for this opportunity to colloborate with fellow cohort members and faculty, as well as tackling such an interesting and large (in scope) project.
